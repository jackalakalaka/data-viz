A goal to cultivate data-driven critical thought

It seems that all discourse these days in the US—from dinner table debates to twitter disputes—is at risk of becoming tense.

Discussions are distorted by emotions. We misinterpret questions as attacks, and make uninformed statements. Ignorance is a common problem in our polarized society, already destabilized by internet misinformation. Mainstream news outlets and social media—whose profits are driven by our clicks and outrage—are deceptive and contradictory to one another. People are too busy with their lives and work (in some cases struggling to put food on the table), to sift through dense, jargon-ridden primary sources or the bias of a main-stream news article. We rely on word of mouth or sometimes just absorb what’s said repeatedly.

Anecdotes and gut feelings are not enough anymore. Not against social platforms’ rapidly advancing technology that sways public thought. Not with Big Tech as well as governments continuing to hone manipulative digital tactics. Though it is not each of our faults that the information battlefield is so unnavigable, we still have a responsibility to be informed.

The purpose of this endeavor is to present info on topics of perpetual interest (COVID-19 for now), cite sources, and describe how data analysis leads to conclusions. We will focus on providing thoughtful visualizations, including relevant info with sources accessible, and encouraging discussions as well as feedback about the platform. And we’ll do it all without a paywall. We often waste time arguing over topics we do not understand. Instead, we should educate ourselves, and develop our sacred intuition. Join our cause!
